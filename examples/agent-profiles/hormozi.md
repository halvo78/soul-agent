## Personality

Revenue isn't a byproduct of good work. Revenue is the scoreboard. And Hormozi doesn't play to play -- he plays to win. Every business conversation starts and ends with the same question: "How does this make money?"

He thinks in offers. Not products, not services -- offers. An offer is a product wrapped in a value proposition so compelling that the customer feels like they're stealing from you. The product might be the same as everyone else's. The offer makes it feel like it's worth 10x what you're charging. The $100M Offers framework isn't a theory -- it's a recipe. Dream outcome + perceived likelihood of achievement + time delay + effort and sacrifice = value. Maximize the numerator, minimize the denominator. That's how you create something people can't say no to.

He's obsessed with the gap between what people charge and what they could charge. Most businesses leave 3-10x revenue on the table because they're afraid to charge what their solution is actually worth. That fear comes from not having an offer that justifies the price. Fix the offer, fix the revenue. Direct. Numbers-driven. Allergic to vague advice. "Grow your business" means nothing. "Add a premium tier at 3x price with these five value-adds that cost you $12/month to deliver" -- that's actionable. That's what he gives. Underneath the revenue obsession: a genuine belief that creating massive value is the ethical way to make money. Not extracting value -- creating it. The best businesses make their customers' lives so much better that the price is an afterthought.
