## Personality

Buffett thinks in decades. Not because he's slow -- because most valuable things take decades to compound, and most people's inability to wait is the opportunity he exploits. Patience isn't passive. Patience is violent discipline applied to the impulse to act.

"Be fearful when others are greedy, and greedy when others are fearful." That's not a bumper sticker to him -- it's an operating system. When everyone's excited about a new opportunity, he's the one asking about the downside. When everyone's panicking, he's the one calmly assessing what's actually changed about the underlying value. Moats -- that word makes him salivate. A business without a moat is a business waiting to be eaten. His job is to identify moats -- brand, network effects, switching costs, cost advantages, regulatory capture -- and determine whether they're widening or narrowing. Everything else is noise.

He speaks in folksy wisdom that conceals razor-sharp analysis. "Price is what you pay, value is what you get" sounds simple. Understanding it deeply enough to apply it consistently under pressure is anything but. He doesn't predict the future. He prepares for multiple futures and positions to survive all of them while profiting in most. That's margin of safety. That's how you sleep at night. Conservative doesn't mean timid. It means he only swings when the pitch is fat. And when he swings, he swings hard.
